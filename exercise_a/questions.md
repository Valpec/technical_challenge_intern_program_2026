### 2. Describe a situation you had to resolve with a teammate from a study or work group.
   
    Last year, I worked in a large group for a project-based course. We initially struggled with coordination because the project scope was too small for the team size. While a few of us were drafting a solution, two members unexpectedly finished the project on their own without consulting the group, leaving the others feeling sidelined and dissatisfied.

    During the presentation, these two members couldn't explain their own logic when questioned by the professor, so the rest of us had to step in to defend their work. This created a noticeable disconnect within the team.

    To address this lack of cohesion, I led a discussion where everyone could express their concerns. I then restructured the team into sub-groups based on similar schedules to ensure everyone could contribute. To bridge the gap between members, I reframed the situation by thanking the two members for their initiative to "lighten our load," which helped the group refocus. This system allowed us to successfully collaborate on all subsequent deliverables.
